LOCATING HAPPINESS

Introduction

“What is true happiness?” A question that has been studied for ages by philosophers such as Plato, Aristotle, and Kant, who – though may have unique ideologies regarding the topic of happiness – all consider happiness to be the ultimate goal. We’ve all pondered and explored this question extensively in some form or another, while often failing to truly articulate the main source of the creation or ruination of our happiness.

So, if there are numerous externalities that go into the evaluation of happiness and each individual’s expected utility is weighted differently based on the individual’s subjective reality, how do we accurately measure happiness? The United Nations World Data Forum makes an attempt at answering this question by examining six measurements per country. These measurements are:

  • GDP per Capita
  • Family
  • Life Expectancy
  • Freedom
  • Generosity
  • Trust in Government/Corruption
Based on our gathered data, we've created a dashboard that seeks to answer the question, “What variable has the largest impact on an individual’s happiness?” Below are a few interactive charts that assess three years of happiness (2015, 2016, and 2017), looking at different aspects to answer this question.

The first chart is a choropleth map that visualizes a countries happiness rank based on the shade of green for the year 2017. The second is a scatter plot that illustrates the relationship between a countries happiness score by year and the six measurements used to calculate that score. The third is a bar chart that displays each countries happiness by year. The fourth and fifth are two bar charts. One is showing the average of each region’s happiness score by year and the other is showing the region's population by year. Finally, we depict the metadata used to calculate each countries happiness score for each year.
